AI access is becoming a public-policy question, not just a product subscription.

OpenAI and the Government of Malta announced a partnership to make ChatGPT Plus available for one year at no cost to Maltese citizens who complete the national AI for All literacy course developed by the University of Malta.

What makes this notable is the combination of access and education:
- Citizens first complete a practical AI literacy course.
- The Malta Digital Innovation Authority will manage distribution to eligible participants.
- The programme is positioned as part of OpenAI for Countries, focused on national AI adoption rather than one-size-fits-all deployment.

Why it matters: broad AI adoption will depend less on giving people tools alone, and more on building confidence, responsible use, and practical skills across society. Malta is turning that into a national programme — and it will be worth watching whether other governments follow.
