Enterprise AI is moving from experimentation into infrastructure design.

At Dell Technologies World, Dell and NVIDIA framed the next phase of enterprise AI around agentic workloads: secure agents running close to company data, from deskside workstations to on-premises AI factories and confidential computing environments.

A few signals stood out:

- Dell estimates worldwide AI infrastructure spending could reach $3–4T by 2030, with token consumption growing 3,400% over the same period.
- The new Dell PowerEdge XE9812, built on NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72, is positioned for up to 10× lower cost per token than Blackwell for large-scale agentic inference.
- NVIDIA Vera CPU systems are described as completing agentic workloads 50% faster than x86 processors, with 1.2 TB/s memory bandwidth for data-heavy agent pipelines.

Why it matters: enterprise agents are not just a model problem. They need compute, data access, governance, privacy, networking, and runtime controls. The Dell/NVIDIA stack is another sign that “AI platforms” are becoming full infrastructure products, not just APIs or copilots.
