## Key thesis

AI is a "co-intelligence" — not just a tool but a collaborative partner that augments human thinking. Mollick argues AI is a General Purpose Technology (like steam power or the internet) but with faster adoption and deeper impact on cognitive work. He proposes practical frameworks for integrating AI into creative work, education, business, and personal development.

## Context

Ethan Mollick is a professor at Wharton School (UPenn), known for his newsletter "One Useful Thing" and early research on AI in education and business. The book synthesizes his hands-on experimentation and academic research into actionable guidance.
